Classic Kung Fu

Iconic films from the golden age of Hong Kong martial arts, emphasizing traditional techniques and choreography.

Enter the Dragon (1973)

Overview

Bruce Lee infiltrates a crime lord’s island tournament, delivering electrifying fights and raw charisma. A genre-defining masterpiece, though the plot’s simple. Rating: 9/10.

Drunken Master (1978)

Overview

A young Jackie Chan masters a quirky fighting style with boozy flair. Playful and inventive, it’s a kung fu classic with heart. Rating: 8/10.

Fist of Fury (1972)

Overview

Bruce Lee avenges his master in a fiery tale of nationalism and vengeance. Intense fights, but the story leans on melodrama. Rating: 8/10.

The Way of the Dragon (1972)

Overview

Bruce Lee faces off in Rome, culminating in an iconic Colosseum showdown. Raw and thrilling, though the pacing drags at times. Rating: 8/10.

The Big Boss (1971)

Overview

Bruce Lee’s breakout sees him smashing a crime ring. Gritty action, but the story’s thin and dated. Rating: 7/10.

Shaolin Wooden Men (1976)

Overview

A mute monk trains to face wooden dummies and foes. Jackie’s early work shines, but the plot’s bare-bones. Rating: 7/10.

Snake in the Eagle’s Shadow (1978)

Overview

Jackie Chan learns a unique style to fend off rivals. Fun and fluid choreography, though the story’s predictable. Rating: 7/10.

The Young Master (1980)

Overview

Jackie Chan hunts a fugitive with acrobatic flair. Energetic fights, but the narrative’s a bit sloppy. Rating: 7/10.

Game of Death (1978)

Overview

Bruce Lee battles up a tower, but posthumous edits dilute his magic. Iconic yellow suit, weak story. Rating: 6/10.

Game of Death II (1981)

Overview

A cobbled-together sequel using Lee’s leftovers. Some decent fights, but it’s a shameless cash grab. Rating: 5/10.

Shaolin Invincible Sticks (1978)

Overview

A monk wields sticks in a revenge tale. Solid fights, but the low-budget story lacks punch. Rating: 5/10.

The Secret Rivals (1976)

Overview

Rival fighters team up against a villain. Decent action, but generic plot and dated production hold it back. Rating: 5/10.

Four Infernos to Cross (1981)

Overview

A martial artist faces deadly trials. Obscure and low-budget, with fights that barely spark. Rating: 4/10.

Guards of Shaolin (1984)

Overview

Shaolin monks protect a sacred relic. Routine fights and a thin story make it forgettable. Rating: 4/10.

Revenge of the Shaolin Kid (1978)

Overview

A young fighter seeks vengeance. Standard kung fu fare with passable action but little else. Rating: 4/10.

To Kill with Intrigue (1977)

Overview

Jackie Chan in a convoluted revenge plot. Some creative fights, but the melodrama drags it down. Rating: 4/10.

Modern Kung Fu

Contemporary takes on martial arts, often blending traditional styles with modern storytelling.

Ip Man (2008)

Overview

A Wing Chun master defends honor during Japan’s invasion. Elegant fights and Donnie Yen’s gravitas make it a modern classic. Rating: 8/10.

Kung Fu Hustle (2004)

Overview

A goofy wannabe gangster unleashes wild martial arts chaos. Stephen Chow’s blend of slapstick and stunning fights is pure genius. Rating: 8/10.

Fearless (2006)

Overview

Jet Li’s Huo Yuanjia rises as a martial arts legend. Stirring fights and patriotism, though the drama’s a bit heavy. Rating: 7/10.

Fist of Legend (1994)

Overview

Jet Li remakes Fist of Fury with slick fights and vengeance. Sharp choreography, but the story’s a retread. Rating: 7/10.

Ip Man 2 (2010)

Overview

Ip Man faces colonial foes and rival masters. Great action, but the nationalism leans heavy. Rating: 7/10.

The Forbidden Kingdom (2008)

Overview

Jackie Chan and Jet Li team up in a mythical quest. Fun fights, but the Westernized plot feels forced. Rating: 7/10.

The Iron Monkey (1977)

Overview

A masked hero fights corruption with kung fu flair. Solid action, but the story’s a bit rote. Rating: 7/10.

Ip Man 3 (2015)

Overview

Ip Man battles gangsters and a boxer. Strong fights, but the family drama feels tacked on. Rating: 6/10.

Kung Fu Jungle (2014)

Overview

A martial artist hunts a killer targeting fighters. Gritty action, but the plot’s a standard thriller. Rating: 6/10.

Legend of the Fist: Return of Chen Zhen (2010)

Overview

Donnie Yen revisits a Bruce Lee hero in wartime. Stylish fights, but the story’s a patriotic rehash. Rating: 6/10.

Man of Tai Chi (2013)

Overview

Keanu Reeves directs a tai chi fighter’s descent into underground bouts. Sleek action, but the story’s thin. Rating: 6/10.

Master Z: Ip Man Legacy (2018)

Overview

A Wing Chun master faces triads and foreigners. Decent fights, but it lacks Ip Man’s soul. Rating: 6/10.

True Legend (2010)

Overview

A warrior seeks revenge after betrayal. Flashy fights, but the overblown plot dilutes the impact. Rating: 6/10.

Chinese Zodiac (2012)

Overview

Jackie Chan hunts artifacts with martial arts flair. Fun but formulaic, it’s a lesser entry in his catalog. Rating: 5/10.

Ip Man 4: The Finale (2019)

Overview

Ip Man takes on America’s racists. Action’s solid, but the series ends with a tired, preachy note. Rating: 5/10.

Legend of the Red Dragon (1994)

Overview

Jet Li protects his son in a period brawler. Decent fights, but the melodrama and pacing sag. Rating: 5/10.

Ip Man: Kung Fu Master (2019)

Overview

A prequel with Ip Man as a cop. Lackluster fights and a generic story make it a weak spinoff. Rating: 4/10.

Action-Comedy Martial Arts

Films blending martial arts with humor, often featuring Jackie Chan’s signature stunts.

Rush Hour (1998)

Overview

Jackie Chan and Chris Tucker’s buddy-cop chaos delivers laughs and slick fights. A cultural gem, though the plot’s thin. Rating: 8/10.

Shaolin Soccer (2001)

Overview

Stephen Chow’s monks use kung fu to dominate soccer. Absurdly funny and visually wild, it’s a genre-bending riot. Rating: 8/10.

Operation Condor (1991)

Overview

Jackie Chan hunts treasure with death-defying stunts. High-energy fun, but the plot’s a chaotic afterthought. Rating: 7/10.

Project A: Part II (1987)

Overview

Jackie Chan battles pirates and corruption with acrobatic brilliance. Stunts dazzle, but the story’s a bit messy. Rating: 7/10.

Rumble in the Bronx (1995)

Overview

Jackie Chan fights New York gangs with wild stunts. Pure fun, though the dubbed plot’s gloriously silly. Rating: 7/10.

Rush Hour 2 (2001)

Overview

Chan and Tucker bicker through another crime caper. Fun chemistry and fights, but it’s a familiar sequel. Rating: 7/10.

Kung Pow: Enter the Fist (2002)

Overview

A parody dubbing old kung fu footage with absurd humor. Niche and ridiculous, it’s a cult hit for some. Rating: 6/10.

Rush Hour 3 (2007)

Overview

Chan and Tucker chase triads in Paris. The charm’s still there, but the formula’s wearing thin. Rating: 6/10.

The Medallion (2003)

Overview

Jackie Chan gains supernatural powers via a mystical artifact. Fun stunts, but the fantasy plot’s a mess. Rating: 6/10.

The Tuxedo (2002)

Overview

Jackie Chan’s suit grants superpowers in a spy caper. Goofy fun, but the gimmick outshines the fights. Rating: 6/10.

Twin Dragons (1992)

Overview

Jackie Chan plays twins in a chaotic comedy. Decent stunts, but the slapstick plot’s a bit dated. Rating: 5/10.

Beverly Hills Ninja (1997)

Overview

Chris Farley’s bumbling ninja stumbles through a goofy plot. Silly laughs, but the martial arts are a joke. Rating: 4/10.

Hollywood Martial Arts

Martial arts films with Western leads or heavy Western influence, often blending with action or drama.

The Last Samurai (2003)

Overview

A Westerner learns samurai ways in a clash of cultures. Epic and respectful, though it romanticizes history. Rating: 8/10.

The Karate Kid (1984)

Overview

A teen learns karate and life lessons from a wise mentor. Nostalgic and heartfelt, though a bit formulaic. Rating: 7/10.

The Last Dragon (1985)

Overview

A kung fu dreamer seeks “the glow” in a funky blaxploitation tribute. Cheesy charm and solid fights make it a cult hit. Rating: 7/10.

Karate Kid: Legends (2024)

Overview

A new kid trains under martial arts masters to face personal and competitive challenges. Solid choreography with a fresh cast, but it leans heavily on franchise nostalgia. Rating: 6/10.

Kiss of the Dragon (2001)

Overview

Jet Li fights corrupt cops in Paris. Brutal action, but the gritty plot’s a bit generic. Rating: 6/10.

Romeo Must Die (2000)

Overview

Jet Li’s in a gang war with a romantic twist. Cool fights, but the story’s a cliché-ridden slog. Rating: 6/10.

The Karate Kid Part II (1986)

Overview

Daniel and Miyagi face foes in Okinawa. Decent action, but it leans hard on the original’s formula. Rating: 6/10.

Unleashed (2005)

Overview

Jet Li’s a caged fighter finding humanity. Raw action and heart, but the drama’s overly sentimental. Rating: 6/10.

The Karate Kid (2010)

Overview

A kid learns kung fu in China with Jackie Chan. Solid remake, but it lacks the original’s charm. Rating: 5/10.

The Next Karate Kid (1994)

Overview

Miyagi trains a new student in a tired sequel. Some heart, but the spark’s gone. Rating: 5/10.

The Karate Kid Part III (1989)

Overview

Daniel faces a vengeful foe in a weak sequel. Forced drama and repetitive fights dim the legacy. Rating: 4/10.

Ninja and Assassin Films

Martial arts films focusing on stealth, ninjas, or assassins with intense combat.

Ninja Assassin (2009)

Overview

A rogue ninja battles his clan with bloody flair. Gory action thrills, but the story’s a shallow cut. Rating: 6/10.

Naked Weapon (2002)

Overview

Female assassins fight with style in a sleazy thriller. Decent action, but the exploitative plot grates. Rating: 5/10.

Ninja: The Final Duel (1986)

Overview

Ninjas clash in a low-budget spectacle. Campy fights, but the story’s a disjointed mess. Rating: 4/10.

Thai Martial Arts

Films showcasing Muay Thai and other Thai fighting styles with intense, raw action.

Ong Bak: Muay Thai Warrior (2003)

Overview

Tony Jaa hunts a stolen relic with jaw-dropping Muay Thai. Raw and relentless, though the story’s basic. Rating: 7/10.

Ong Bak 2 (2008)

Overview

Tony Jaa’s warrior seeks revenge in a period epic. Stunning fights, but the plot’s a chaotic mess. Rating: 6/10.

The Protector (2005)

Overview

Tony Jaa chases stolen elephants with brutal Muay Thai. Insane action, but the story’s a flimsy excuse. Rating: 6/10.

Wuxia and Historical

Martial arts films with a focus on historical settings or wuxia’s poetic, mythical combat.

Crouching Tiger, Hidden Dragon: Sword of Destiny (2016)

Overview

A warrior protects a legendary sword in a wuxia sequel. Graceful fights, but it lacks the original’s soul. Rating: 8/10.

My Father Is a Hero (1995)

Overview

Jet Li’s cop fights triads to save his son. Emotional and action-packed, though the drama’s a bit sappy. Rating: 7/10.

Tai-Chi Master (1993)

Overview

Jet Li’s monk masters tai chi in a wuxia tale. Lively fights, but the story’s a bit overstretched. Rating: 7/10.

Shaolin & Wu Tang (1983)

Overview

Rival schools clash in a wuxia showdown. Solid choreography, but the plot’s a standard feud. Rating: 6/10.

Kids and Family Martial Arts

Lighthearted martial arts films aimed at younger audiences or with a playful tone.

3 Ninjas (1992)

Overview

Kid ninjas fight bumbling crooks. Nostalgic kid fun, but the action and humor are dated. Rating: 5/10.

3 Ninjas Kick Back (1994)

Overview

The ninja kids head to Japan for a dagger quest. Goofy but weaker than the first, it’s pure 90s cheese. Rating: 4/10.

3 Ninjas Knuckle Up (1995)

Overview

Kid ninjas battle polluters. Slapstick and silly, it’s a forgettable sequel for fans only. Rating: 4/10.

3 Ninjas: High Noon at Mega Mountain (1998)

Overview

The ninja kids face theme park terrorists. A low point for the series, it’s lazy and uninspired. Rating: 3/10.

Magnificent 7 Kung-Fu Kids (1989)

Overview

Kid fighters take on crime in a low-budget romp. Charming for kids, but the action’s weak and dated. Rating: 3/10.

Other Martial Arts

Miscellaneous martial arts films with unique styles or settings, often experimental or niche.

The Master (1992)

Overview

Jet Li’s kung fu master fights in America. Decent action, but the cultural clash plot’s clunky. Rating: 6/10.

Rumble in Hong Kong (1973)

Overview

Jackie Chan’s early cop role in a gritty crime flick. Raw but unpolished, it’s for completists only. Rating: 5/10.

The Crippled Masters (1979)

Overview

Disabled fighters team up for revenge. Unique and bold, but the execution’s rough and exploitative. Rating: 5/10.

The Heroine (1973)

Overview

A female fighter takes on drug lords. Gritty for its time, but the low budget and weak plot show. Rating: 5/10.

The Ring of Death (1980)

Overview

A fighter enters a deadly tournament. Obscure with some cool fights, but the story’s a generic slog. Rating: 5/10.

Who Am I? (1998)

Overview

Jackie Chan’s amnesiac agent fights for answers. Great stunts, but the convoluted plot drags. Rating: 5/10.

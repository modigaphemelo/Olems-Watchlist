Classic Bond

007’s golden era of martinis, gadgets, and world-saving swagger.

Casino Royale (2006)

Overview

Craig’s gritty Bond reboots with poker-faced intensity and parkour chases. Some miss the camp, but this raw, emotional 007 deals a royal flush. Rating: 9/10.

Skyfall (2012)

Overview

Bond hunts a cyber-ghost while facing MI6’s past. Naysayers nitpick the plot holes, but Mendes’ sleek visuals and Bardem’s creepy flair make it a license to thrill. Rating: 8/10.

Goldfinger (1964)

Overview

Connery’s Bond foils a gold-obsessed baddie with laser beams and Aston Martins. Dated sexism stings, but the suave charm and iconic lines are pure bullion. Rating: 8/10.

From Russia with Love (1963)

Overview

Bond navigates Cold War traps and a deadly femme fatale. Some call it slow, but the gritty espionage and train-fight grit keep it a vodka-soaked classic. Rating: 8/10.

GoldenEye (1995)

Overview

Brosnan’s Bond battles a rogue 007 with tanks and tech. Dated effects and cheesy quips irk some, but the N64 nostalgia and action zing keep it golden. Rating: 7/10.

Dr. No (1962)

Overview

Connery’s debut Bond hunts a Caribbean madman. Pacing drags for modern eyes, but the suave swagger and exotic vibe launch the franchise with a bang. Rating: 7/10.

No Time to Die (2021)

Overview

Craig’s final Bond faces a bio-weapon baddie and personal ghosts. Bloated runtime and soap-opera vibes irk fans, but the emotional farewell lands some punches. Rating: 6/10.

The Spy Who Loved Me (1977)

Overview

Moore’s Bond teams with a Soviet stunner to sink a sub-stealing villain. Campy excess annoys purists, but the Lotus sub and Jaws chomp hard. Rating: 6/10.

Thunderball (1965)

Overview

Bond dives for stolen nukes in a Bahamas showdown. Waterlogged pacing drowns some, but the underwater action and Connery’s cool keep it afloat. Rating: 6/10.

For Your Eyes Only (1981)

Overview

Moore’s Bond chases a missile gizmo with ski stunts and grit. Uneven tone trips some, but the grounded spy game and action zing keep it sharp. Rating: 6/10.

Live and Let Die (1973)

Overview

Moore’s Bond dives into voodoo and drug-lord danger. Blaxploitation clichés and campy boats irk, but the bayou vibe and Wings theme song slink along. Rating: 5/10.

Spectre (2015)

Overview

Bond hunts a shadowy cabal tying his past. Fans slam the forced retcons and dull villain, but Craig’s grit and slick setpieces save it from self-destruct. Rating: 5/10.

You Only Live Twice (1967)

Overview

Bond fakes death to stop a volcano-lair villain. Goofy excess and dated tropes irk, but the ninja action and Connery’s charm keep it twice as nice. Rating: 5/10.

Diamonds Are Forever (1971)

Overview

Connery’s back to chase diamonds and Blofeld. Silly Vegas vibes and tired gags dim the shine, but the glitzy action still sparkles faintly. Rating: 5/10.

Never Say Never Again (1983)

Overview

Connery’s rogue Bond redo of Thunderball feels creaky. Fans hate the knockoff vibe, but the elder 007’s charm keeps this non-canon caper watchable. Rating: 5/10.

The Man with the Golden Gun (1974)

Overview

Moore’s Bond hunts a hitman with a shiny pistol. Cartoonish villains and dated kung-fu flop, but Lee’s icy menace gives it a golden glint. Rating: 5/10.

Tomorrow Never Dies (1997)

Overview

Brosnan’s Bond battles a media mogul’s war plot. Generic action and weak villains bore, but Yeoh’s kicks and boat chases keep it from sinking. Rating: 5/10.

Quantum of Solace (2008)

Overview

Bond’s revenge quest chases a water-hoarding creep. Choppy editing and thin plot tank it, but Craig’s feral energy keeps this shaken, not stirred. Rating: 4/10.

Licence to Kill (1989)

Overview

Dalton’s dark Bond goes rogue for drug-lord revenge. Gritty shift alienates fans of camp, but the raw action makes it a bloody outlier. Rating: 4/10.

The World Is Not Enough (1999)

Overview

Brosnan’s Bond guards oil heiresses from a scarred assassin. Messy plot and weak twists fizzle, but the action and Dench’s M keep it ticking. Rating: 4/10.

A View to a Kill (1985)

Overview

Moore’s aging Bond battles Walken’s psycho tech bro. Creaky pacing and absurd plots crash, but the villain’s cackle keeps it from total wipeout. Rating: 3/10.

Octopussy (1983)

Overview

Bond chases nukes through circuses and Indian intrigue. Goofy title and silliness bomb, but Moore’s charm and exotic stunts juggle some fun. Rating: 3/10.

Moonraker (1979)

Overview

Bond blasts to space for a Star Wars knockoff. Ludicrous lasers and campy excess crash-land, but the sheer audacity keeps it oddly watchable. Rating: 3/10.

Die Another Day (2002)

Overview

Brosnan’s Bond surfs through CGI ice and invisible cars. Fans shred the cartoonish excess, but the sword fight and Berry’s strut give it faint pulse. Rating: 3/10.

Modern Spy Thrillers

Gritty, grounded espionage with high stakes and higher body counts.

The Bourne Identity (2002)

Overview

Amnesiac assassin Bourne outruns his past with shaky-cam swagger. Some balk at the grit shift, but Damon’s intensity and tight action redefine spy cool. Rating: 8/10.

The Bourne Supremacy (2004)

Overview

Bourne’s revenge chase amps the stakes and car crashes. Complex plot confuses some, but Greengrass’ kinetic chaos keeps it a pulse-pounding win. Rating: 8/10.

Atomic Blonde (2017)

Overview

Theron’s MI6 badass kicks Cold War ass in neon Berlin. Style-over-substance gripes linger, but the bone-crunching fights and '80s vibe slay. Rating: 7/10.

Safe House (2012)

Overview

Washington’s rogue spy schools a CIA rookie in Cape Town chaos. Generic twists bore some, but Denzel’s charisma and gritty action keep it locked. Rating: 6/10.

Salt (2010)

Overview

Jolie’s CIA agent dodges double-crosses in a twisty sprint. Absurd plot holes irk, but the relentless pace and Angie’s steel keep it salty. Rating: 6/10.

Spy Comedy

Spoofs and lighthearted takes on espionage antics.

Spy (2015)

Overview

McCarthy’s desk-jockey spy bumbles into badassery. Some tire of the fat jokes, but the sharp gags and action-comedy zing make it a covert hoot. Rating: 7/10.

Austin Powers: The Spy Who Shagged Me (1999)

Overview

Groovy Austin time-travels to foil Dr. Evil’s moon base. Juvenile gags wear thin, but Myers’ manic energy and '60s spoof keep it shagadelic. Rating: 7/10.

Johnny English (2003)

Overview

Atkinson’s bumbling spy saves Britain from a French plot. Slapstick misses some marks, but Bean’s pratfalls and goofy charm keep it covertly fun. Rating: 6/10.

Central Intelligence (2016)

Overview

Hart’s accountant joins The Rock’s rogue spy for laughs and bullets. Formulaic buddy shtick drags, but the odd-couple chemistry sparks just enough. Rating: 6/10.

Johnny English Reborn (2011)

Overview

English fumbles a global conspiracy with kung-fu monks. Recycled gags bore, but Atkinson’s rubber-faced antics keep this sequel from total wipeout. Rating: 5/10.

Johnny English Strikes Again (2018)

Overview

English bungles a cyberattack probe with analog antics. Tired formula and weak laughs crash, but Atkinson’s earnest idiocy ekes out a chuckle. Rating: 4/10.

The Spy Next Door (2010)

Overview

Chan’s spy babysits kids while dodging Russian goons. Clichéd family fluff flops, but Jackie’s stunts and charm keep this kiddie caper kicking. Rating: 4/10.

Stylized Spy Action

Over-the-top espionage with comic-book flair and wild visuals.

Kingsman: The Secret Service (2014)

Overview

A street kid joins posh spies to stop a tech tyrant. Ultraviolent excess splits fans, but Vaughn’s slick style and Firth’s suave carnage pop off. Rating: 8/10.

Kingsman: The Golden Circle (2017)

Overview

Eggsy teams with Yanks to fight a drug-lord diva. Bloated chaos and tonal whiplash irk, but the bonkers action keeps this sequel sharply dressed. Rating: 6/10.

The King's Man (2021)

Overview

WWI prequel births the Kingsman with historical hijinks. Uneven tone and weird history lessons flop, but Fiennes’ class and war action keep it dapper. Rating: 6/10.

XXX (2002)

Overview

Diesel’s extreme spy snowboard-fights anarchists. Dated X-Games vibes and cheesy lines crash, but the stunt-heavy chaos keeps it barely rad. Rating: 5/10.

XXX: Return of Xander Cage (2017)

Overview

Diesel’s back for more stunt-spy nonsense. Brainless excess and weak plot nosedive, but the global crew and absurd action scrape a pulse. Rating: 4/10.

Female-Led Spy

Badass women flipping the espionage game with style.

Charlie's Angels (2000)

Overview

Trio of glam spies kick butt for a mystery boss. Campy fluff and dated pop vibes irk, but Diaz, Barrymore, and Liu’s charisma keep it angelically fun. Rating: 6/10.

Charlie's Angels (2019)

Overview

New Angels fight tech baddies with girl-power gusto. Forced feminism and weak script crash, but Stewart’s spark keeps this reboot from total sabotage. Rating: 4/10.

Kid-Friendly Spy

Family-safe espionage with pint-sized heroes.

Spy Kids (2001)

Overview

Kids save their spy parents with wacky gadgets. Over-the-top silliness bugs adults, but Rodriguez’s colorful chaos is a kid’s covert dream. Rating: 6/10.

Spy Kids 2: The Island of Lost Dreams (2002)

Overview

Spy sibs battle mutants and rival kids on a weird island. Cluttered plot loses steam, but the quirky gadgets keep young agents entertained. Rating: 5/10.

Stormbreaker (2006)

Overview

Teen spy Alex Rider tackles a tech villain. Generic YA vibes and low stakes flop, but the breezy action keeps this junior Bond barely afloat. Rating: 4/10.

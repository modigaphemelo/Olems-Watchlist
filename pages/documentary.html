Music Legends and Biographies

Explorations into the lives and legacies of iconic musicians who shaped genres and culture.

Searching for Sugar Man (2012)

Overview

A ghost of folk music, unknown in America yet a revolutionary icon in South Africa. This soul-stirring hunt for a lost artist unveils a poetic enigma whose melodies toppled apartheid’s walls. Gripping, but the mystery’s reveal feels almost too perfect. Rating: 9/10.

What Happened, Miss Simone? (2015)

Overview

Nina Simone’s voice carried civil rights fire, her piano a weapon against injustice. This raw portrait of genius and torment hits like a protest song, though it lingers heavily on her pain. Rating: 9/10.

Marley (2012)

Overview

Bob Marley’s reggae heartbeat pulsed with rebellion and hope. From Trenchtown to global stardom, this deep dive into his life grooves hard but stretches long, like a jam session past midnight. Rating: 8/10.

Standing in the Shadows of Motown (2002)

Overview

The Funk Brothers, Motown’s secret sauce, laid down grooves that defined a generation. This tribute to unsung heroes swings with soul, though it’s more nostalgic than revelatory. Rating: 8/10.

Chasing Trane (2016)

Overview

John Coltrane’s saxophone wailed with spiritual hunger, reshaping jazz. This tribute captures his genius but feels like a love letter that doesn’t dig deep enough. Rating: 7/10.

It Might Get Loud (2008)

Overview

Three guitar gods—Page, White, Edge—riff on their craft. It’s a sonic love-in, but the jam session vibe overshadows deeper insights into their artistry. Rating: 7/10.

Jimi Hendrix - Hear My Train a Comin' (2013)

Overview

Hendrix’s guitar set the ‘60s ablaze, a psychedelic comet gone too soon. This doc strums his legend well, but it’s more a mixtape than a profound revelation. Rating: 7/10.

Biggie - The Life of Notorious B.I.G. (2017)

Overview

Biggie’s rhymes painted Brooklyn’s grit and glamour, his life cut short by violence. This doc flows smoothly but leans too hard on hagiography over hard truths. Rating: 7/10.

The Black Godfather (2019)

Overview

Clarence Avant, music’s behind-the-scenes titan, brokered deals and broke barriers. This doc celebrates his influence but glosses over the messier edges of power. Rating: 6/10.

True Crime and Scandals

Investigations into shocking crimes, exploitation, and hidden injustices that rocked society.

I Am Evidence (2017)

Overview

Unprocessed rape kits pile up, a silent indictment of justice denied. This gut-punch doc exposes systemic failure while championing survivors’ fight for change. Sobering but slightly repetitive. Rating: 8/10.

Hot Girls Wanted (2015)

Overview

The amateur porn industry lures young women with promises of fame, only to chew them up. This unflinching look is grimly compelling but feels exploitative itself at times. Rating: 7/10.

Our Father (2022)

Overview

A fertility doctor’s twisted secret spawns dozens of siblings—and a chilling betrayal. This doc grips like a thriller but leans too heavily on sensationalism. Rating: 6/10.

Money Shot - The Pornhub Story (2023)

Overview

Pornhub’s rise and scandals expose the internet’s wild west of desire. Intriguing but scattered, it probes ethics without landing a decisive blow. Rating: 6/10.

Holy Heist (2022)

Overview

A preacher, a robbery, and a wild tale of faith-fueled crime. This doc tries for holy shock value but stumbles with a messy narrative and thin depth. Rating: 5/10.

Social Issues and Political Figures

Examinations of societal challenges, philosophical ideas, and influential thinkers shaping discourse.

Citizenfour (2014)

Overview

Edward Snowden pulls back the curtain on global surveillance, a real-time thriller of secrets and stakes. Tense and vital, though its intensity can feel claustrophobic. Rating: 9/10.

Every Brilliant Thing (2016)

Overview

A one-man show turns a list of life’s joys into a shield against darkness. Heartfelt and inventive, it’s a bittersweet hug that occasionally leans too theatrical. Rating: 8/10.

To End All War - Oppenheimer & the Atomic Bomb (2023)

Overview

Oppenheimer’s atomic legacy is a dance of genius and guilt. This doc dissects the bomb’s birth thoughtfully, but it’s overshadowed by flashier retellings. Rating: 7/10.

Thomas Sowell - Common Sense in a Senseless World (2021)

Overview

Sowell’s contrarian intellect slices through dogma with data. This doc admires his clarity but feels like a fan club lecture, lacking critical balance. Rating: 6/10.

Examined Life (2008)

Overview

Philosophers muse on life’s big questions amid city streets. Thought-provoking but meandering, it’s like eavesdropping on a lecture hall with no syllabus. Rating: 6/10.

The Disruptors (2022)

Overview

ADHD gets a rebrand as a creative superpower. Uplifting but overly polished, it champions neurodiversity while sidestepping deeper systemic critiques. Rating: 6/10.

Dick - The Documentary (2013)

Overview

Men talk frankly about their anatomy and identity. Bold in concept, but the execution feels gimmicky, more curious than profound. Rating: 5/10.

Screened Out (2020)

Overview

Social media’s addictive trap gets a surface-level exposé. It raises alarms about screen time but lacks the depth to truly unplug you. Rating: 5/10.

Science, Technology, and Exploration

Stories of innovation, discovery, and the human quest to push boundaries.

Return to Space (2022)

Overview

SpaceX rockets NASA back to the stars with Elon’s audacious vision. Slick and inspiring, but it’s more corporate PR than cosmic revelation. Rating: 6/10.

Automotive Adventures

Thrilling journeys and challenges on the road, celebrating the spirit of exploration.

Top Gear - The Great African Adventure (2013)

Overview

Top Gear’s trio tackle Africa’s wild roads in beat-up cars, delivering laughs and chaos. It’s a joyride, but the colonial lens grates at times. Rating: 7/10.

Top Gear The Perfect Road Trip (2013)

Overview

Supercars and scenic routes fuel Top Gear’s banter-heavy quest for the ultimate drive. Fun but formulaic, it’s petrolhead candy with little substance. Rating: 7/10.

Top Gear The Perfect Road Trip 2 (2014)

Overview

More Top Gear antics, chasing the perfect drive with shiny cars and cheeky quips. Entertaining, but it’s a retread that doesn’t top the original. Rating: 7/10.

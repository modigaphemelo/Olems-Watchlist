Documentaries on Cybercrime

Real-life explorations of hacking, cyber warfare, and digital underworlds.

Billion Dollar Heist (2023)

Overview

Digital bandits pull off a crypto caper that shakes the blockchain world. Gripping dive into dark web dealings, though the tech jargon might crash your system. Rating: 7/10.

Cyberbunker - The Criminal Underworld (2023)

Overview

A bunker hosts servers for the internet’s shadiest schemes. Fascinating peek into cybercrime’s fortress, but the narrative occasionally buffers. Rating: 7/10.

TPB AFK - The Pirate Bay - Away from Keyboard (2013)

Overview

Pirate Bay’s founders navigate courtroom storms over their torrent empire. Raw and rebellious, though the legal tangles can feel like a download stuck at 99%. Rating: 7/10.

We Are Legion - The Story of the Hacktivists (2012)

Overview

Anonymous unmasks the power of hacktivism in a digital uprising. Chaotic and eye-opening, but the anonymity blurs the focus at times. Rating: 6/10.

Zero Days (2016)

Overview

Stuxnet’s cyber sabotage exposes the chilling reality of digital warfare. Tense and revelatory, it’s a code-breaking thriller that doesn’t need a sequel. Rating: 8/10.

Fictional Hacking Thrillers

Dramatic tales of cybercrime, espionage, and digital intrigue.

Blackhat (2015)

Overview

A convicted coder hunts a digital phantom wreaking global havoc. Gritty tech-thriller vibes, but the keyboard action clacks louder than the plot. Rating: 6/10.

Situational

Hacker (2016)

Overview

A desperate coder dives into the dark web to fund his family’s dreams. Ambition meets ambition, but the stakes feel as repetitive as a phishing scam. Rating: 5/10.

Hackers (1995)

Overview

Teen tech rebels hack their way out of a corporate frame job. 90s nostalgia pops with neon flair, though the tech looks more retro than radical now. Rating: 6/10.

WarGames (1983)

Overview

A teen’s modem mischief nearly triggers nuclear armageddon. Cold War tension meets early internet charm, still clicking despite dated dial-up vibes. Rating: 7/10.

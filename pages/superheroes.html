Marvel Cinematic Universe

Films from the interconnected Marvel universe, featuring iconic heroes and sprawling team-ups.

Avengers: Endgame (2019)

Overview

Heroes unite for a time-bending showdown to undo cosmic devastation, delivering epic stakes and emotional closure. A monumental spectacle, though it leans hard on fan service. Rating: 9/10.

Avengers: Infinity War (2018)

Overview

A galactic tyrant snaps his fingers, and heroes scramble to save reality itself. Jaw-dropping clashes, but the sprawling cast can overwhelm. Rating: 8/10.

Black Panther (2018)

Overview

A king defends his futuristic African nation, blending cultural pride with high-tech action. Vibrant and groundbreaking, though the villain steals the show. Rating: 8/10.

Captain America: Civil War (2016)

Overview

Ideals clash as heroes pick sides in a superhero showdown, with loyalty and duty tearing friends apart. Thrilling, but it’s practically an Avengers movie. Rating: 8/10.

Captain America: The Winter Soldier (2014)

Overview

A super-soldier uncovers a shadowy conspiracy, blending espionage with explosive action. Tight and thrilling, though the stakes feel familiar. Rating: 8/10.

Guardians of the Galaxy (2014)

Overview

Misfit space rogues band together to save the galaxy, fueled by a killer soundtrack and irreverent humor. Cosmic fun, though the plot’s a bit standard. Rating: 8/10.

Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 2 (2017)

Overview

The ragtag crew explores family ties and cosmic threats, with more laughs and heart. Flashy and fun, but slightly overstuffed. Rating: 8/10.

Guardians of the Galaxy Vol. 3 (2023)

Overview

The Guardians face their darkest mission yet, with emotional depth and wild visuals. A fitting finale, though it’s heavy on sentiment. Rating: 8/10.

Iron Man (2008)

Overview

A genius playboy builds a high-tech suit to fight evil, launching a cinematic universe with wit and swagger. Iconic start, though it’s light on depth. Rating: 8/10.

Spider-Man: Homecoming (2017)

Overview

A young web-slinger balances teen life and heroics under Iron Man’s wing. Fresh and fun, though it leans hard on MCU connections. Rating: 8/10.

Thor: Ragnarok (2017)

Overview

A thunder god teams up with misfits to save Asgard in a colorful, comedic cosmic romp. Vibrant and hilarious, though the stakes feel light. Rating: 8/10.

Avengers: Age of Ultron (2015)

Overview

Heroes battle a rogue AI bent on global domination, with quips and chaos aplenty. Ambitious but messy, it’s a middle chapter that sags. Rating: 7/10.

Black Panther: Wakanda Forever (2022)

Overview

Wakanda mourns and fights to protect its legacy against a new aquatic threat. Emotional and bold, but overstretched with grief. Rating: 7/10.

Captain America: The First Avenger (2011)

Overview

A scrawny kid becomes a WWII super-soldier, fighting Nazis and finding purpose. Earnest and nostalgic, but a bit formulaic. Rating: 7/10.

Captain Marvel (2019)

Overview

A cosmic warrior uncovers her Earth-bound past, blasting through alien conspiracies. Empowering but predictable, with 90s nostalgia as a bonus. Rating: 7/10.

Doctor Strange (2016)

Overview

A surgeon turned sorcerer bends time and reality to save the world. Mind-bending visuals, but the origin story treads familiar ground. Rating: 7/10.

Doctor Strange in the Multiverse of Madness (2022)

Overview

Magic and multiversal chaos collide as a sorcerer faces dark temptations. Stylish and wild, but the plot’s a tangled spell. Rating: 7/10.

Iron Man 2 (2010)

Overview

Tony Stark faces rivals and his own demons while juggling new tech and enemies. Fun but cluttered, it’s a sequel with spark but no shine. Rating: 7/10.

Shang-Chi and the Legend of the Ten Rings (2021)

Overview

A martial artist confronts his shadowy past and a mystical legacy. Dynamic fights and rich lore, though it leans on formula. Rating: 7/10.

The Avengers (2012)

Overview

Earth’s mightiest heroes unite to fend off an alien invasion. Explosive team-up thrills, but it’s more flash than depth. Rating: 7/10.

Ant-Man (2015)

Overview

A thief shrinks to save the day with tiny tech and big heart. Light and quirky, but it’s small-scale in ambition too. Rating: 6/10.

Ant-Man and the Wasp (2018)

Overview

Shrinking heroes tackle a quantum heist with zippy action and humor. Breezy fun, but it’s forgettable fluff. Rating: 6/10.

Iron Man 3 (2013)

Overview

Tony battles trauma and a fiery foe while grounded. Introspective but uneven, with a twist that divides fans. Rating: 6/10.

Thor (2011)

Overview

A brash god learns humility on Earth while facing cosmic kin. Mythic but clunky, it’s a rocky start for the thunderer. Rating: 6/10.

Ant-Man and the Wasp: Quantumania (2023)

Overview

Tiny heroes get lost in a quantum mess, facing a multiversal menace. Ambitious visuals, but the story’s a chaotic speck. Rating: 5/10.

Thor: The Dark World (2013)

Overview

Thor battles dark elves to save the cosmos, but the plot’s murkier than the title. Forgettable filler with some cool effects. Rating: 5/10.

The Incredible Hulk (2008)

Overview

A scientist flees his green alter-ego while hunted by the military. Decent action, but it’s a hulk-sized missed opportunity. Rating: 5/10.

DC Extended Universe

Films from DC’s cinematic universe, showcasing gritty heroes and mythic conflicts.

Wonder Woman (2017)

Overview

An Amazon warrior battles in WWI, blending myth with heart-pounding action. Empowering and epic, though the finale’s a bit generic. Rating: 8/10.

Aquaman (2018)

Overview

An ocean king claims his throne in a splashy underwater epic. Bold visuals, but the story’s as shallow as a tide pool. Rating: 7/10.

Shazam! (2019)

Overview

A kid transforms into a goofy superhero, embracing powers with childlike glee. Fun and heartfelt, but light on stakes. Rating: 7/10.

Man of Steel (2013)

Overview

Superman’s origin gets a gritty reboot, with city-leveling battles and moral weight. Visually grand, but overly somber. Rating: 6/10.

Aquaman and the Lost Kingdom (2023)

Overview

Aquaman dives back into action to save his kingdom, with more underwater flair. Colorful but cluttered, it’s a soggy sequel. Rating: 6/10.

Shazam! Fury of the Gods (2023)

Overview

The Shazam family faces divine wrath in a chaotic sequel. Playful but overstuffed, it’s a lightning bolt that fizzles. Rating: 6/10.

Batman v Superman: Dawn of Justice (2016)

Overview

Two titans clash in a grim, overstuffed epic, setting up a universe that stumbles. Ambitious but muddled, with a dour tone. Rating: 5/10.

Black Adam (2022)

Overview

An antihero unleashes ancient power, but generic action and a thin plot dim the spark. Loud and forgettable. Rating: 5/10.

Suicide Squad (2016)

Overview

Villains team up for a chaotic mission, with style but no substance. Flashy visuals can’t save a jumbled mess. Rating: 5/10.

Batman Legacy

Standalone and series-based films centered on Gotham’s Dark Knight across different eras.

The Dark Knight (2008)

Overview

Gotham’s gritty superhero saga where chaos wears a purple suit. Explosive action, moral dilemmas, and a pencil trick you’ll never forget. A bit overlong, but it’s the hero we deserve. Rating: 9/10.

Batman Begins (2005)

Overview

Bruce Wayne becomes the Dark Knight, forging his crusade against crime. Grounded and intense, though it takes time to soar. Rating: 8/10.

The Batman (2022)

Overview

A brooding detective hunts a sadistic killer in a noir-drenched Gotham. Atmospheric and gripping, though it lingers too long. Rating: 8/10.

The Dark Knight Rises (2012)

Overview

Batman faces a brutal foe to save Gotham from ruin. Epic but bloated, it closes the trilogy with ambition if not precision. Rating: 7/10.

Batman Returns (1992)

Overview

Gotham’s caped crusader battles a feline femme fatale and a waddling crime lord. Quirky and dark, but overstuffed with villains. Rating: 6/10.

Batman (1989)

Overview

A gothic take on the Dark Knight facing a manic clown. Iconic for its time, but dated and uneven today. Rating: 5/10.

Batman & Robin (1997)

Overview

Campy chaos with ice puns and neon excess, derailing the franchise with cartoonish glee. A frosty misfire. Rating: 3/10.

Spider-Man 2 (2004)

Overview

Peter Parker juggles heroics and heartbreak while battling a multi-armed menace. Heartfelt and thrilling, it’s a web-spinning classic. Rating: 8/10.

Spider-Man (2002)

Overview

A teen gains powers and fights crime, learning responsibility the hard way. Nostalgic and earnest, though a bit dated. Rating: 7/10.

The Amazing Spider-Man (2012)

Overview

A rebooted Spidey tackles a lizardly foe and personal mysteries. Fresh and stylish, but it retreads familiar webs. Rating: 7/10.

Spider-Man 3 (2007)

Overview

Peter battles symbiotes and inner demons, but too many villains clog the web. Ambitious but overstuffed. Rating: 6/10.

X-Men Universe

Mutant heroes and villains clash in a world fearing their powers.

X-Men: Days of Future Past (2014)

Overview

Mutants time-travel to prevent a dystopian future, blending eras with high stakes. Thrilling and smart, though complex. Rating: 8/10.

Logan (2017)

Overview

A weathered Wolverine protects a young mutant in a brutal, heartfelt farewell. Gritty and emotional, it’s a claw-some swan song. Rating: 8/10.

X-Men: First Class (2011)

Overview

Young mutants forge alliances and rivalries in a 60s-set origin. Stylish and engaging, though it’s a bit safe. Rating: 7/10.

X2 (2003)

Overview

Mutants unite against a genocidal plot, with action and heart. Strong sequel, though it shows its age. Rating: 7/10.

X-Men (2000)

Overview

Mutants battle prejudice and a magnetic villain in a groundbreaking start. Dated but pivotal for the genre. Rating: 6/10.

The Wolverine (2013)

Overview

Logan faces samurai and immortality in Japan. Gritty action, but the story’s a bit of a claw-back. Rating: 6/10.

X-Men: Apocalypse (2016)

Overview

Mutants face an ancient god, but bloated stakes and generic chaos dim the spark. Ambitious but underwhelming. Rating: 5/10.

X-Men: The Last Stand (2006)

Overview

Mutants clash over a cure, but rushed plotting and character missteps weaken the impact. A messy finale. Rating: 5/10.

X-Men: Dark Phoenix (2019)

Overview

A mutant’s cosmic power spirals out of control, but lackluster execution dims the fire. A flat farewell. Rating: 4/10.

X-Men Origins: Wolverine (2009)

Overview

Logan’s past unravels in a cluttered origin story. Some fun action, but it’s a choppy mutant mess. Rating: 4/10.

Other Superhero Films

Standalone or non-franchise superhero stories, including antiheroes and unique takes.

Deadpool (2016)

Overview

A wisecracking mercenary seeks revenge with bullets and chimichangas. Raunchy, irreverent, and wildly entertaining. Rating: 8/10.

Deadpool 2 (2018)

Overview

The merc with a mouth teams up for more meta mayhem and heart. Crude and chaotic, it’s a bloody good time. Rating: 8/10.

Deadpool & Wolverine (2024)

Overview

Two regenerators team up for a multiversal buddy comedy with guts and gags. Nostalgic and nutty, it’s a stab-happy blast. Rating: 8/10.

Hancock (2008)

Overview

A flawed superhuman cleans up his act and his city. Fresh take on heroics, but the second half stumbles. Rating: 7/10.

Superman (1978)

Overview

The Man of Steel saves the world with old-school charm. Groundbreaking for its time, though it feels dated now. Rating: 7/10.

Superman 2 (1980)

Overview

Superman faces Kryptonian foes and a romantic dilemma. Classic but creaky, it’s a nostalgic power-up. Rating: 7/10.

Teenage Mutant Ninja Turtles (1990)

Overview

Pizza-loving turtles fight crime with martial arts flair. Cheesy fun, but the low budget shows. Rating: 6/10.

Daredevil (2003)

Overview

A blind vigilante fights crime with heightened senses. Gritty ideas, but clunky execution and dated vibes. Rating: 5/10.

Fantastic Four (2005)

Overview

A cosmic family battles a metallic foe. Light and campy, but lacks the spark of true heroism. Rating: 5/10.

Fantastic Four: Rise of the Silver Surfer (2007)

Overview

The Fantastic Four face a cosmic herald, but bland plotting dims the shine. Flashy but forgettable. Rating: 5/10.

Venom (2018)

Overview

A journalist bonds with a gooey symbiote for chaotic heroics. Fun but messy, it’s a tonal rollercoaster. Rating: 5/10.

Bloodshot (2020)

Overview

A resurrected soldier uncovers a tech-fueled conspiracy. Generic action and weak stakes make it forgettable. Rating: 4/10.

Green Lantern (2011)

Overview

A pilot wields a cosmic ring, but bad effects and a thin plot dim the glow. A galactic misfire. Rating: 4/10.

The Darkest Minds (2018)

Overview

Teens with powers fight a dystopian regime. Intriguing setup, but generic execution and weak follow-through. Rating: 4/10.

Morbius (2022)

Overview

A scientist becomes a vampire in a lifeless, incoherent slog. Muddled plot and zero pulse—it’s a cinematic bloodbath. Rating: 1/10.

Tech Biopics

Stories of computer pioneers and their revolutionary impact.

The Social Network (2010)

Overview

Zuckerberg’s meteoric rise building Facebook weaves betrayal and genius. Sorkin’s sharp script crackles, though some call its dramatization exaggerated and cold. Rating: 8/10.

Steve Jobs (2015)

Overview

Three pivotal Apple launches frame Jobs’ brilliance and flaws. Fassbender’s electric turn shines, but stagey structure and relentless dialogue alienate some. Rating: 7/10.

Steve Jobs: The Man in the Machine (2015)

Overview

A documentary probes Jobs’ legacy, from innovation to ruthless ambition. Insightful archival footage informs, but biased lens and lack of focus dilute its byte. Rating: 6/10.

Tech Industry & Innovation

Chronicles of the computer world’s rise and its unsung heroes.

Silicon Cowboys (2016)

Overview

Compaq’s underdog battle against IBM in the PC wars sparks nostalgia. Engaging storytelling captivates, but limited scope and dry delivery boot some viewers. Rating: 7/10.

Revolution OS (2001)

Overview

The open-source movement’s rise, led by Linux pioneers, gets a geeky spotlight. Informative for techies, but dated visuals and niche focus crash for casual audiences. Rating: 6/10.

The Code (2001)

Overview

A documentary dives into Linux’s creation and open-source ethos. Passionate interviews intrigue, but low production values and repetitive preaching log off mainstream appeal. Rating: 6/10.

Social Media & Digital Culture

Exploring the societal impact of the digital age.

The Social Dilemma (2020)

Overview

Tech insiders expose social media’s addictive, polarizing dark side. Alarming insights hit hard, but dramatized segments and preachy tone buffer its signal. Rating: 7/10.

Tech Thrillers

High-stakes dramas in the world of hacking and corporate espionage.

AntiTrust (2001)

Overview

A coder battles a sinister tech mogul stealing open-source ideas. Timely paranoia entertains, but dated tech and cartoonish villains crash its credibility. Rating: 6/10.

I.T. (2016)

Overview

A tech consultant hacks a CEO’s life in a digital vendetta. Brosnan’s gravitas tries, but generic plotting and absurd tech tropes unplug the suspense. Rating: 5/10.

CTRL (2018)

Overview

A gamer’s virtual world blurs with reality as code turns deadly. Intriguing indie premise fizzles with low-budget execution and a muddled, unscary script. Rating: 4/10.

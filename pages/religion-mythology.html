Biblical Retellings

Stories inspired by or reimagining biblical narratives and figures.

Exodus: Gods and Kings (2014)

Overview

Moses frees the Israelites in a dour, overblown epic. Stunning visuals, but the soul of the story’s lost in the sand. Rating: 4/10.

Noah (2014)

Overview

Noah builds an ark amid divine visions. Ambitious but muddled, it drowns in its own grim spectacle. Rating: 5/10.

Paul, Apostle of Christ (2018)

Overview

Paul preaches from a Roman prison amid persecution. Solemn but plodding, it’s more devotional than dramatic. Rating: 6/10.

The Book of Clarence (2023)

Overview

A hustler in Jesus’ time fakes divinity for clout. Bold and irreverent, but its satire stumbles under uneven pacing. Rating: 6/10.

Supernatural and Apocalyptic

Films blending religion with supernatural forces, demons, or end-of-world stakes.

Constantine (2005)

Overview

A demon hunter battles hell’s forces in a supernatural noir. Stylish and grim, but the religious lore feels like comic book cosplay. Rating: 6/10.

End of Days (1999)

Overview

A cop takes on Satan to stop the apocalypse. Schwarzenegger’s grit can’t save this overblown, cheesy biblical thriller. Rating: 3/10.

I, Frankenstein (2014)

Overview

Frankenstein’s monster fights demons in a divine war. Ludicrous and dull, it’s a gothic mess with no soul. Rating: 3/10.

Legion (2010)

Overview

Angels wage war on humanity, and a diner crew fights back. Messy and absurd, it’s a theological B-movie gone awry. Rating: 4/10.

The Devil Conspiracy (2023)

Overview

A cult clones Jesus to summon Satan. Ridiculous and sloppy, it’s a B-movie with delusions of grandeur. Rating: 3/10.

The Devil's Advocate (1997)

Overview

A lawyer works for the Devil in a slick firm. Juicy premise, but the campy excess overshadows the moral sting. Rating: 6/10.

The Book of Eli (2010)

Overview

A wanderer guards a sacred book in a ruined world. Stylish but predictable, the twist feels forced and cheap. Rating: 5/10.

Faith and Redemption

Stories centered on personal journeys of faith, salvation, or spiritual transformation.

An Interview with God (2018)

Overview

A journalist interviews a man claiming to be God. Stagy and sanctimonious, it’s more lecture than revelation. Rating: 4/10.

Do You Believe? (2015)

Overview

Interwoven faith stories preach to the choir with heavy-handed drama. It’s earnest but clunky, more pamphlet than film. Rating: 4/10.

Father Stu (2022)

Overview

A boxer becomes a priest, chasing redemption. Gritty but overly sentimental, it’s a predictable faith journey. Rating: 5/10.

God’s Not Dead (2014)

Overview

A student debates an atheist professor about God. Smug and simplistic, it’s propaganda posing as drama. Rating: 3/10.

God’s Not Dead 2 (2016)

Overview

A teacher fights a lawsuit over mentioning Jesus. More preachy than the first, it’s a courtroom sermon, not a movie. Rating: 3/10.

Heaven Is for Real (2014)

Overview

A boy’s visions of heaven stir his family’s faith. Cloying and manipulative, it’s a Hallmark card in movie form. Rating: 4/10.

Miracles from Heaven (2016)

Overview

A girl’s miracle recovery fuels family faith. Sappy and contrived, it’s engineered for tears, not insight. Rating: 4/10.

The Case for Christ (2017)

Overview

An atheist journalist tries to debunk Christianity. Stiff and agenda-driven, it’s more tract than tale. Rating: 5/10.

The Preacher's Wife (1996)

Overview

An angel aids a preacher’s faltering marriage. Warm but dated, it coasts on charm instead of depth. Rating: 6/10.

Satire and Comedy

Films using humor or satire to explore religion and its institutions.

Bruce Almighty (2003)

Overview

A whiny reporter gets God’s powers and botches divine duties. Carrey’s charm carries it, but the theology’s as deep as a kiddie pool. Rating: 6/10.

Evan Almighty (2007)

Overview

A congressman builds an ark for God. Slapstick drowns out any depth, leaving a soggy, forgettable comedy. Rating: 5/10.

Honk for Jesus. Save Your Soul. (2022)

Overview

A megachurch couple rebounds from scandal in a satire. Bites but doesn’t chew, fizzling out with uneven humor. Rating: 6/10.

Life of Brian (1979)

Overview

A man mistaken for the Messiah mocks religious dogma. Still sharp, though some gags age less gracefully than others. Rating: 7/10.

The Invention of Lying (2009)

Overview

A man invents religion in a truth-only world. Clever but softens into a sappy rom-com, wasting its bold premise. Rating: 6/10.

The Man Who Sued God (2001)

Overview

A man sues God over a lightning strike. Fun idea, but the execution’s as flat as a bad sermon. Rating: 4/10.

Religulous (2008)

Overview

Maher mocks religion in a cheeky documentary. Provocative but one-sided, it’s more snark than substance. Rating: 6/10.

Yes, God, Yes (2019)

Overview

A Catholic teen navigates desire at a strict retreat. Honest but unremarkable, it’s a familiar coming-of-age tale. Rating: 7/10.

Mythology and Fantasy

Films rooted in ancient myths or fantastical interpretations of divine stories.

Clash of the Titans (2010)

Overview

Perseus fights gods and beasts in a mythological mess. All flash, no substance—Greek myths deserve better than this CGI slog. Rating: 4/10.

Gods of Egypt (2016)

Overview

A mortal aids gods against a tyrant deity in Egypt. Gaudy CGI and a messy plot sink this mythological misfire. Rating: 3/10.

The Legend of Hercules (2014)

Overview

Hercules fights for his destiny in a mythic dud. Flat acting and cheap effects sink this wannabe epic. Rating: 3/10.

Institutional Religion

Films examining the structures, conflicts, or leaders of religious institutions.

The Da Vinci Code (2006)

Overview

A symbologist uncovers a Jesus conspiracy. Pacy but overstuffed, it’s a thriller that thinks it’s deeper than it is. Rating: 5/10.

The Two Popes (2019)

Overview

Popes Benedict and Francis clash over faith and reform. Engaging but polished, it’s more cozy than challenging. Rating: 8/10.

Three Christs (2017)

Overview

A doctor studies three patients claiming to be Jesus. Curious but sluggish, it raises questions it doesn’t answer. Rating: 5/10.
